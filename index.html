Why we built this

The truth about the company
shouldn't live in five different tools.

Dashboards in one app, tasks in another, numbers in a spreadsheet, meetings in a doc, decisions in a chat thread. Every tool is fine on its own — and the company is still blind, because nothing is connected.

Before — scattered tools
  • ✕Leaders inspect every row manually to find what needs attention.
  • ✕Scores are missing and nobody notices until the review meeting.
  • ✕Work has no owner, no due date, no status — just activity.
  • ✕The weekly meeting starts with "so… where are we?" for 40 minutes.
  • ✕Files, decisions and context are scattered across chats and drives.
After — one connected workspace
  • ✓Leadership by exception — dashboards surface what needs attention; leaders don't hunt for it.
  • ✓Truth before activity — missing entries, red Scorecards, overdue tasks and off-track Rocks are highlighted first.
  • ✓Ownership over ambiguity — every work object has an owner, due date and status.
  • ✓The WorkFlow Syncs Meeting opens on the scoreboard — numbers first, explanations second.

In one sentence: WorkFlow is a company operating system for organizing branches, Scorecards, quarterly priorities, issues, projects, daily work, knowledge and meeting records — in one connected workspace.
Product philosophy

Six rules the product never breaks.

WorkFlow doesn't treat dashboards, tasks, Scorecards and meetings as separate tools. It connects them — and these six principles decide every design choice.

01

Truth before activity

Missing Scorecard entries, red numbers, overdue tasks, open issues, off-track Rocks and stale projects are surfaced first — so teams know where to look before anyone talks.

02

Ownership over ambiguity

Every work object carries an owner, a due date, a status, a department and a role. If nobody owns it, it isn't real work yet.

03

Personal work first

My Work is the member's home: assigned tasks, project work, personal Scorecards, owned Rocks and raised issues — one queue, no hunting.

04

Leadership by exception

Company and workspace dashboards surface the items that need attention, instead of forcing leaders to inspect every row manually.

05

Context stays attached

Files, channels, issues, projects and meeting records stay connected to the work they explain. Nothing important lives only in someone's head — or someone's chat.

06

One operating language

Scorecards, Rocks, Issues, Projects, My Work, Team Work, WorkFlow Syncs Meeting. Seven words, used the same way by everyone — from HQ to the newest member.

The thinking behind it

One unbroken line,
from purpose to today's task.

WorkFlow is built on a simple belief: if you can't trace a task all the way up to the company's purpose, the task shouldn't exist. The app mirrors how a real company cascades — HQ sets it, the unit owns it, the department measures it, the individual does it.

Company / HQ
Company Rock
"Grow enrollment 40% this quarter." Set at HQ. The company command view watches unit health, off-track Rocks and red Scorecards across every unit.
Branch / Business Unit
Unit Rock
"Yangon campus: add 80 new students." Each unit gets its own workspace — Dashboard, Scoreboard, Rocks, Issues, Projects, Meetings, Members.
Department
Measurable Goal
"Book 120 consultations, convert 80." Departments group members, own Scorecards, and make the Rock a number someone can hit.
Individual
My Work — today
"Call 15 leads. Update my Scorecard. Close 2 tasks." The level where strategy finally becomes daily action — in one personal queue.

↓ Cascade down

Purpose becomes a goal, the goal becomes Rocks, Rocks become projects and tasks. Each level makes the one above it concrete — nothing is vague by the time it reaches a person.

↑ Roll up

Tasks complete projects. Projects complete Rocks. Unit results roll into company health — and the HQ command view shows the top of the stack, live, without anyone writing a report.

Make work visible at the right level. Keep ownership explicit. Move every conversation toward action.

The operating flow

From setup to a running company, in seven steps.

This is the order in which a company comes alive inside WorkFlow.

Set up the workspace. Create the company, invite members, assign roles, create departments where useful.

Define Scorecards and owners so the team knows exactly which numbers must be updated every week.

Create quarterly Rocks connected to owners, due dates, status and the Scorecards they should move.

Plan the work. Projects for structured execution; Team Work for short daily actions.

Members run from My Work — keeping tasks, Scorecards, Rocks and raised issues current in one queue.

Leaders inspect exceptions on the dashboards and run the WorkFlow Syncs Meeting from the meeting hub.

Everything becomes the record. Issues, actions, files, channels and meeting history stay available as the company's operating memory.

The meeting opens on the scoreboard

No status theatre. The board already shows what's green, what's red and what's missing — so the conversation starts where the truth is, not where the loudest voice is.

IDS turns talk into action

Identify the real problem, Discuss the root cause, Solve it with one owner and one date. No issue leaves the room without all three. Solutions become tasks in the workspace — automatically part of next week's truth.

The record compounds

Attendance, ratings, solved issues and meeting history stay in the system. Every week the company gets a little harder to confuse and a little easier to run.
